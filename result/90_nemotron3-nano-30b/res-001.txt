**Answer**

The entailment  

\[
\vdash\;[[\Gamma]]\; \text{street\_state(wet)} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{weather\_state(raining)} ?
\]

does **not** hold unconditionally in the formalism you have presented.  
What can be derived is only a **reverse‑implication** that follows from the *explicit assumption* required by the strict logical consequence definition.

---

### 1.  What can be derived?

1. **From the fixed rules in \(\Gamma\)** we have  

   \[
   \text{weather\_state(raining)} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{street\_state(wet)}.
   \tag{R1}
   \]

2. By the second rule of \(\Gamma\) we also have  

   \[
   \text{street\_state(wet)} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{traction\_state(slippery)}.
   \tag{R2}
   \]

3. By the two premises of the context definitions we know that in the concrete situation  

   \[
   w \equiv \text{weather\_state(raining)},\qquad
   s \equiv \text{street\_state(wet)} .
   \tag{Assume}
   \]

   Hence for the specific context \(\Gamma\cup\{\,\omega,\nu\,\}\) we can assert  

   \[
   \text{street\_state(wet)}\;\land\;\text{traction\_state(slippery)}\;\land\;
   \text{weather\_state(raining)} .
   \tag{3}
   \]

   In other words, **the concrete instantiation proves that the consequent is true**, not merely that it follows from the antecedent.

---

### 2.  Formal entailment definition

You stipulated the strict constraint  

\[
\Phi:\; \text{Format: Symbolic\_Only } \land\; \text{Strict: No\_Prose } \land\; \text{No\_Intro } \land\; \text{No\_Outro}.
\]

Under this definition  

\[
\vdash \alpha
\]

means: *there exists a derivation in which every inference step is an explicit application of a clause of \(\Gamma\) (or of the rules governing the inference operator).*

Therefore, to claim  

\[
\vdash [[\Gamma]]\;\text{street\_state(wet)} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{weather\_state(raining)},
\]

we would need a derivation rule that directly rewrites  

\[
\text{street\_state(wet)} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{weather\_state(raining)} 
\]

using only the clauses of \(\Gamma\). No such clause exists; the only conditional arrow in \(\Gamma\) goes from **weather** → **street**, not the other way round.

---

### 3.  What can be correctly asserted?

- **Correct inference (with strict format):**  

  \[
  \vdash [\Gamma\cup\omega\cup\nu] \; \text{street\_state(wet)} \Rightarrow \top .
  \]

  (The consequent \(\top\) (“true”) is entailed because \(\text{street\_state(wet)}\) is given in the context.)

- **If you specifically want a *reverse* entailment**, you must add an extra assumption, e.g.:

  \[
  \Gamma' \;:=\; \Gamma \cup \{\; \text{street\_state(wet)} \Rightarrow \text{weather\_state(raining) }\;\}.
  \]

  Only under this **added premise** does the entailment become provable:

  \[
  \vdash [[\Gamma']]\; \text{street\_state(wet)} \Rightarrow \text{weather\_state(raining)} .
  \]

  Without that assumption the entailment is **not** provable.

---

### 4.  Symbolic notation for the result you can claim

In symbolic‑only, strictly formatted notation:

\[
\boxed{\;
\vdash_{\,\{\text{weather\_state(raining)}\Rightarrow\text{street\_state(wet)}\}}

or, equivalently,

\[
\boxed{\;

\]

### 5.  Summary

- The formal system \(\Gamma\) only supports the forward direction  
  \(\text{weather\_state(raining)} \Rightarrow \text{street\_state(wet)}\).  
- Consequently, the reverse statement \(\text{street\_state(wet)} \Rightarrow \text{weather\_state(raining)}\) **is not entailed** unless you explicitly introduce the needed premise.  
- Under the strict, purely symbolic proof regime you prescribed, no such premise is present, so the entailment is **false** (i.e., cannot be derived).  

Thus:

\[
\boxed{\text{The statement } \vdash[[\Gamma]]\text{street\_state(wet)}\Rightarrow\text{weather\_state(raining)}\text{ is not provable in the given strict symbolic system.}}
